# Insights and Recommendations

## Key Insights

### 1. Applicant volume was high, but final hiring was limited
The dataset shows **5,134 total applicants** and **382 hired candidates**, resulting in an overall hiring rate of **7.44%**.

This suggests the funnel attracted a large number of candidates, but only a small share reached the final hiring stage.

### 2. The biggest drop-off happened in the early funnel
The funnel narrowed the most between the **Applied** and **Screened** stages.

This indicates that early-stage filtering removed a large portion of applicants before they moved deeper into the hiring process.

### 3. Conversion improved as candidates moved further in the funnel
Conversion rates improved for candidates who moved deeper into the funnel.

Candidates who reached interview and offer stages had better chances of moving forward compared to the earlier stages of the funnel.

### 4. Offer-to-hire conversion was very strong
The final conversion from **Offer** to **Hired** was about **97.45%**.

This suggests that once candidates received offers, most of them were successfully hired.

### 5. Job Fair, LinkedIn, and Indeed brought the highest hires
Among recruitment sources, **Job Fair**, **LinkedIn**, and **Indeed** generated the highest number of hired candidates.

This shows that these sources were the strongest contributors to final hiring outcomes.

### 6. Time taken increased at later stages
Average time spent in the process increased as candidates moved across the funnel.

Later stages such as **Offer** and **Hired** took more time than early stages like **Screened**, showing a gradual increase in process duration.

---

## Recommendations

### 1. Review early-stage screening criteria
Since the biggest drop-off happens early in the funnel, the screening process should be reviewed to check whether too many candidates are being filtered out at the start.

### 2. Focus more on high-performing recruitment sources
Job Fair, LinkedIn, and Indeed can be treated as stronger sourcing channels because they contributed the highest hires.

Recruitment effort and budget can be prioritized more toward these sources.

### 3. Monitor weaker mid-funnel transitions
Although final-stage conversion is strong, earlier and mid-stage conversion still affects the total number of hires.

Tracking stage-level movement more closely can help identify where additional improvement is needed.

### 4. Reduce delays in later hiring stages
Since the time taken increases in later stages, the hiring team can review approval, interview scheduling, and offer processing steps to improve overall hiring speed.

### 5. Track funnel performance regularly
Regular tracking of applicant counts, conversion rates, source effectiveness, and stage timelines can help recruiters improve hiring efficiency over time.
